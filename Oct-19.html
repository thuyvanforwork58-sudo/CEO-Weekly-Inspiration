The Tipping Point: Keep Going Until It Clicks
October 19, 2025
In this article, CEO Todd Wanek shares how persistence leads to the tipping point ‚Äî the moment when consistent effort transforms into visible results.

Good morning and Happy Gratitude Sunday. I hope you are having an amazing start to your day and focusing on your daily rituals and routines and developing great habits.

I want to talk about something that's probably happened to you before, even if you didn't realize it at the time.

Have you ever worked hard at something - losing weight, learning a skill, breaking a bad habit - and felt like nothing was happening? Week after week, you're doing the work. But the scale isn't moving. The skill isn't clicking. The habit isn't sticking. It's frustrating. You start wondering if maybe it's just not going to work for you.

And then one day, something shifts. The weight starts coming off. The skill clicks. The habit sticks. You're not doing anything different than before - you're doing the same things you've been doing all along. But now, they're working.

The Tipping Point is when all the invisible progress you've been making finally becomes visible. The work was always working. You just couldn't see it yet.

Here's the important part: the tipping point was always coming. Your body was changing before the scale showed it. Your brain was rewiring before the skill felt natural. The habit was forming before it felt automatic. The daily discipline and constant learning were compounding, building toward something you couldn't see yet. The tragedy is when people quit right before the tipping point. They were one week away, one month away from the breakthrough, but because they couldn't see progress, they stopped.

I'm telling you this because many of you are closer to your tipping points than you realize. The daily effort you're putting into your health, your growth, your goals - it's compounding. The small acts of curiosity, the little improvements, the consistent learning - all of it is building toward something. The tipping point is coming. But you must keep going.

Simon Sinek teaches that change doesn't happen evenly across a population. It follows a predictable pattern - a bell curve - and where you are on that curve determines whether you lead or follow.

  • Innovators (2.5%): risk-takers who embrace new ideas immediately.
  • Early Adopters (13.5%): curious, experimental, and ready to build capability before others.
  • Early & Late Majority (68%): wait until ideas are proven and safe.
  • Laggards (16%): resist change until they have no choice.

The tipping point happens at around 15‚Äì18% adoption. Once the Innovators and Early Adopters are on board, the idea tips ‚Äî from risky to normal. After that, everyone follows.

At Ashley, we must be Early Adopters ‚Äî not because it's easy or comfortable, but because that's where advantage is built. Once it tips, the gap becomes impossible to close.
