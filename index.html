CEO WEEKLY INSPIRATION
From "Have To" to "Get To": Embrace Gratitude & Ownership for Extraordinary Impact
October 6, 2025

Team,

I hope this Sunday finds you well and reflecting on the incredible opportunities we have ahead of us. Stick with your daily rituals and routines and become a better version of yourself every day.

This week, I want to share something that completely shifted my perspective, thanks to a book written by one of our own team members, Drew Maddux from Ashley Global Retail. Drew shared his book "Get2" with me, and the story behind it is both powerful and transformative.

When Drew was in college, he was going through a "suffering mindset"-complaining about his classes, his workload, and all the things he felt he "had to" do. During one of his weekly calls with his grandfather, he was venting about how difficult everything was in school and how much he was struggling with his workload and responsibilities.

His grandfather, who was battling cancer and spending many hours each week in chemotherapy, was disappointed in Drew and said something that changed his life forever. He told Drew that he was upset with his attitude because Drew had the incredible privilege of "getting to" do all these things. While his grandfather was fighting just to survive, Drew had the choice to pursue an education, to build his future, and to make an impact. His grandfather reminded him that these weren't burdens-they were gifts.

This story hit me hard because it highlights something we all need to remember, having a mindset of gratitude, growth, and abundance is an amazing gift we can give to ourselves, our families, and our associates.

Think about your own life right now. You don't "have to" come to work-you "get to" contribute to building something incredible. You don't "have to" deal with challenging customers-you "get to" solve problems and create value. You don't "have to" learn new skills-you "get to" expand your capabilities.

This shift from "have to" to "get to" isn't just about positive thinking - it's about recognizing the privilege and opportunity in everything we do.

When you realize you "get to" be part of this team, you "get to" work on meaningful projects, you "get to" make a difference in people's lives through the work we do, it changes everything about how you approach each day.

I also want to share something else that reinforced this message. Suzanne Cisco sent me an article written by her son Kobe about successful people who may have been underachievers in school but had the "motor" to excel because they were hungry for growth. Kobe talks about the importance of "owning" things and being responsible-taking complete ownership of your attitude, your effort, and your results rather than making excuses or blaming circumstances.

This connects perfectly with Drew's message. When you're grateful that you "get to" do something, you naturally take more ownership of it. When you see opportunity instead of obligation, you bring more energy, creativity, and commitment to everything you do.

I challenge each of you this week to change your internal dialogue. Instead of thinking "I have to go to this meeting," think "I get to contribute ideas and help solve problems." Instead of "I have to deal with this difficult situation," think "I get to demonstrate my problem-solving skills and make things better." Instead of "I have to learn this new technology," think "I get to expand my capabilities and become more valuable."

This isn't just about being positive-it's about recognizing the incredible privilege to work on meaningful challenges with an amazing team. While others are struggling just to find employment, we get to be part of something that's growing, innovating, and making a real impact.

You can choose gratitude over complaint. You can choose growth over comfort. You can choose abundance thinking over scarcity thinking.

Most importantly, you can choose to be grateful that you "get to" do things every day - whether they're small tasks or major projects. Be honored that you have these opportunities. Make the right choices in your life and change your "shoulds" to "musts."

Thank you to Drew for sharing his story and book with me, and to Suzanne for sharing Kobe's insights about ownership and having the "motor" to excel. These reminders of perspective and responsibility are exactly what we all need to hear.

Try this: Write one sentence starting with "I get to..." about a task you've been avoiding. Read it aloud. Notice the shift in energy.

Have a great week,

Todd Wanek | CEO
